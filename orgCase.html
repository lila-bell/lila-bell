Case study: People Analytics

A deeper look into organizational initiatives using a combination of R & Excel

This case study analyzes a dataset to assess the impact of organizational initiatives on inclusivity, engagement, and employee retentionton. R is used to determine statistically significant impacts.

Workflow Steps

    Data Preparation

  • Cleaned dataset by identifying and correcting issues such redundant data, removed sensitive information for anonymity and sorted data by employee ID
  • Applied data validation to ensure consistent values and remove outliers (ex. marital status, gender, engagement scores)

    • Recruitment at Diversity Job Fair

  • Cleaned data by removing extra spaces in "M " for males
  • Used COUNTIFS to count and filter male/female employees based on employment status and recruitment method (Diversity Job Fair vs. Traditional)
  • Calculated race frequencies using COUNT and derived percentages for diversity breakdown
  • Performed t-Test comparing job performance and engagement scores between Diversity Job Fair and traditional recruits
  • Found 85% turnover was from traditionally recruited employees, while 15% were Diversity Job Fair recruits

    • Age Diversity Analysis

  • Created PivotTable to calculate mean and standard deviation for employee age
  • Analyzed correlation between age and engagement, finding a negligible correlation coefficient of 0.0633
  • Conducted simple regression to test if age predicts engagement and job performance:
  • R-squared for engagement: 0.002, showing no significant relationship
  • R-squared for performance: 0.004, showing no statistical significance.

    • Engagement and Outcomes

  • Focused on current employees to measure engagement's impact on job performance and intentions to stay
  • Conducted simple regression analysis
  • Engagement to performance: R-squared = 0.309, indicating 30.9% of job performance variability is explained by engagement. p-value < 0.05 , statistically significant
  • Engagement to intentions to stay: R-squared = 0.041, showing a smaller effect but still significant (p-value < 0.05 )
  • Regression coefficients: For performance, each unit increase in engagement led to 0.423 increase in performance; for intentions to stay, each unit increase in engagement led to 0.243 increase in retention

    • Key Insights and Conclusions

  • Gender Breakdown: Slightly more females (56%) than males (44%) in the organization, with similar ratios from the Diversity Job Fair
  • Diversity: 100% of Diversity Job Fair recruits were African American/Black
  • Age Analysis: No significant relationship between age and work engagement or performance
  • Engagement Impact: Strong relationship between engagement and both job performance and intentions to stay
  • Turnover Insights: Diversity Job Fair recruits showed no higher turnover than traditionally recruited employees, indicating successful inclusivity efforts
