Caribbean Currents

This map measures Caribbean ocean surface currents using magnitude and direction. Magnitude is represented in meters per second (m/s) and direction is represented in geographic degrees. < br/> Ocean currents are important for several reasons: 1. Climate regulation: Ocean currents are essential in maintaining Earth's climate by redistributing heat across the globe. For instance, the Gulf Stream transports warm water from the tropics to the North Atlantic, helping to moderate temperatures in Europe. 2. Nutrient distribution: Ocean currents transport nutrients from deep waters to the surface, supporting the growth of phytoplankton and other marine organisms. These organisms form the base of the marine food web and are essential for the survival of many species, including fish and marine mammals. 3. Oxygen production: Phytoplankton, nourished by the nutrients carried by ocean currents, are responsible for generating a substantial portion of the Earth's oxygen through photosynthesis. 4. Marine ecosystems: Ocean currents help determine the distribution of marine species and shape the structure of ecosystems. They create favorable habitats for certain organisms and influence the migration patterns of marine animals. 5. Transportation and trade: Ocean currents have been used for centuries as natural highways for maritime transportation and trade. They can help ships travel more efficiently by taking advantage of favorable currents.
