2020 Voter Turnout by County

Graph Included

Objective

Predict voter turnout by modeling and identifying areas with low participation. This approach aims to increase overall voter participation by developing targeted outreach efforts.

Challenge

To derive specific information about voters through various data engineering tasks. Preparing election result data for predictive analysis requires careful handling of datasets.

Using the Standard Deviation classification method, this choropleth map shows values through a diverging color scheme. Values are categorized based on their devation from the average. Counties with below-average voter turnout are shown in purple, while those with above-average turnout are shown in green.
Underneath the map, the histogram shows the distribution of voter turnout by measuring the frequency at which values appear in the dataset. The x-axis represents different bins of voter turnout values. The y-axis measures the number of counties with voter turnout values falling within each range.

Workflow Steps

  • Cleaned and prepared election data by leveraging ArcNotebooks alongside a suite of Python libraries and Pandas data frames
  • Applied geoenabling and geoenriching methods to incorporate demographic variables for modeling voter turnout
  • Utilized different visualization techniques to explore the relationships between voter turnout and demographic variables
  • Used layer symbology to visualize the spatial distribution of voter turnout across the country
  • Explored variable relationships in a scatterplot matrix

Scatter Plot Matrix

The relationship between voter turnout and other variables are explored in this scatterplot matrix. The mini-plots in the matrix are visualized with a color gradient that corresponds to the strength of the R-squared value. The larger preview plot highlights the relationship between 2020 Voter Turnout and 2016 Voter Turnout. The 2016 Voter Turnout has a positive relationship with 2020's voter turnout, where a higher 2016 Voter Turnout value corresponds to a higher 2020 voter turnout. The R-squared value for this trend is 0.83, which means that the 2016 Voter Turnout alone can explain about 83 percent of the variability in the voter turnout values.
While every pairwise combination of variables is plotted in the matrix, you are specifically interested in how each variable relates to voter turnout. The column of mini-plots on the far left includes the relationships between voter turnout and the other variables.

Datasets: Harvard Dataverse, United States Census Bureau
